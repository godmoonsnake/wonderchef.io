Muya Bai Wahan

Ingredients

  • 500g pork (cut into small pieces)
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots (fresh or canned, sliced)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Water (as needed)

Steps

  1. Prepare the pork:In a large pan, heat mustard oil and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter before adding the chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions are soft and golden.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste:Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Cook the pork:Add the pork pieces to the pan and brown them on all sides. Add turmeric powder and salt. Mix well.
  4. Add bamboo shoots:Stir in the bamboo shoots and mix with the pork. Add enough water to cover the pork and bamboo shoots. Bring to a boil.
  5. Simmer:Reduce the heat and let the dish simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the pork is tender and the flavors have melded together. Add more water if necessary to maintain a saucy consistency.
  6. Serve:Serve hot with steamed rice for a hearty, flavorful meal that reflects the traditional flavors of Tripura.
