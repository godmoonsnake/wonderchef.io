Aam Chana Achar

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw mangoes (peeled and grated)
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas (kabuli chana)
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon nigella seeds (kalonji)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seed powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt (to taste)

Steps

  1. Combine the grated raw mango, turmeric powder, and salt in a deep bowl, mix well, and keep aside for 30 minutes.
  2. Squeeze out all the water from the raw mango into a bowl and keep it aside. Separate the raw mango water and grated raw mango.
  3. Refrigerate the grated raw mango overnight.
  4. In a deep bowl, combine fenugreek seeds, kabuli chana, and raw mango water. Mix well, cover, and keep aside overnight.
  5. In another bowl, combine fenugreek seed powder, fennel seeds, asafoetida, nigella seeds, Kashmiri red chilies, chili powder, grated raw mango, and the kabuli chana-fenugreek seeds mixture. Mix well and keep aside.
  6. Heat mustard oil in a deep non-stick kadhai on a medium flame for 3 to 4 minutes or until red hot.
  7. Remove from the flame and allow the oil to cool completely.
  8. Once cooled, add the mustard oil to the prepared mixture and mix thoroughly.
  9. Bottle the pickle in a sterilized glass jar and keep aside for 6 to 7 days.
  10. After 7 days, the achar is ready to eat. Serve as a tangy accompaniment to your meals.
