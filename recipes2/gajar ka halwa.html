Gajar ka Halwa

Ingredients

  • 6-8 medium-sized carrots (preferably tender and sweet varieties like Red or Desi carrots)
  • 2 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 3 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (cashews, almonds, and pistachios)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder (or to taste)
  • 1 tbsp golden raisins (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp saffron strands (optional, for color and flavor)
  • A pinch of nutmeg (optional)

Steps

  1. Peel and grate the carrots using a fine grater or food processor. You need about 4 cups of grated carrots.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, add the grated carrots and milk. Cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Allow the mixture to simmer until the carrots soften and the milk reduces to almost half (this may take around 25-30 minutes).
  4. Add the sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using). Stir well and continue to cook on low heat, letting the sugar melt and mix evenly.
  5. Heat ghee in a separate pan, and fry the chopped nuts and raisins until golden. Set aside.
  6. Once the milk has reduced, and the mixture is thick, add the fried nuts and raisins to the halwa. Stir everything together.
  7. Cook for another 5-10 minutes until the mixture thickens further and the ghee separates from the halwa.
  8. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with extra nuts or dry fruits as desired.
