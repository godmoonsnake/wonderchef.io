pav bhaji

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (potatoes, cauliflower, peas, carrots)
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons butter (or vegan alternative)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 teaspoons pav bhaji masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 lemon (cut into wedges)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 8 pav buns
  • Chopped onions and extra butter for serving

Steps

  1. Boil the mixed vegetables until soft. Mash them lightly and set aside.
  2. Heat oil and 1 tablespoon of butter in a large pan. Saut√© onions until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, cooking for another minute.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and pulpy.
  5. Stir in pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add the mashed vegetables to the pan and combine with the spice mixture.
  7. Add 1/2 cup of water and simmer, mashing the mixture further to achieve a smooth consistency.
  8. Toast the pav buns on a hot griddle with butter until golden and crispy.
  9. Garnish the bhaji with fresh coriander and a dollop of butter.
  10. Serve hot with the toasted pav, chopped onions, and lemon wedges on the side.
