mysore pak

Ingredients

  • Ghee: 2 tbsp
  • Salt: to taste
  • • Gram flour (besan): 1 cup
  • • Sugar: 2 cups
  • • Ghee: 1 cup
  • • Water: ½ cup

Steps

  1. Prepare the Pan: Grease a square or rectangular tray (or any flat dish) with 2 tablespoons of ghee to prevent the Mysore Pak from sticking.
  2. Roast the Gram Flour: In a heavy-bottomed pan, dry roast the gram flour (besan) over low-medium heat. Stir constantly to avoid burning. Roast it until you get a nutty aroma, about 4-5 minutes. Once done, remove from heat and set it aside.
  3. Make the Sugar Syrup: In another pan, add the sugar and water. Stir it on low-medium heat until the sugar dissolves completely. Then, bring it to a boil. Let it simmer for 3-4 minutes until it reaches a one-string consistency (when you touch the syrup between your thumb and finger, it should form a single thread).
  4. Add Ghee: Add the 1 cup of ghee to the sugar syrup and mix well. The ghee will melt into the syrup. Add a pinch of salt to the mixture (optional, for a slightly savory touch).
  5. Combine Gram Flour with Syrup: Gradually add the roasted gram flour (besan) into the hot sugar and ghee mixture, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. The mixture will thicken, and the ghee will start to separate from the sides.
  6. Cook the Mixture: Keep stirring constantly on low heat. After a few minutes, the mixture will start to come together and form a thick consistency. You’ll know it’s done when it begins to leave the sides of the pan and starts to look like a dough.
  7. Set the Mysore Pak: Pour the mixture into the greased tray and level it using a spatula. Let it cool for 10-15 minutes. Once it sets, cut it into pieces of your desired shape (squares or diamonds).
  8. Serve: Allow the Mysore Pak to cool completely before serving. It will become firm and will have a slightly crumbly texture on the outside, with a soft, melt-in-your-mouth center.
